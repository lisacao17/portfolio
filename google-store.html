Present Day: Designing at Google Store

Full Time UX Designer / Google Store / 2023 - Present

What am I up to?

After graduation, I moved to San Francisco and returned to Google to start my full time role on Google Store. Specifically, I help create delightful and educational experiences for users browsing and shopping our online hardware store.



Thanks to this amazing oppurtunity, I got to work together with super talented team members, perform at I/O with Incognito Mode, and attend an international design conference in South Korea.

Notable Successes

Redesigned our device comparison tools

Although comparison tables are one of the most helpful resources on Google Store, many users frequently expressed pain points on the usability and helpfulness. One of my first projects was to redesign our comparison tools to ensure user-friendliness and improve discoverability. This project recently launched and has shown a ~40% increase in click-through rate, showing a significant increase in use and engagement.



Previous design with unhelpful spec list and lack of engagement


New design with added visuals and interactive elements

Designing new and cost-efficient page templates

Another project that recently launched was a set of page templates that helped save our marketing team $800K in budget. These pages were previously built one-off and customized for each product which was costly and timely. Our new templates create an organized system to improve developmental efficiency and leverages research insights for the best user experience.

Fitbit.com migration

After Google acquired Fitbit.com, we were tasked with the effort of migrating all the products and page content over to our main site. This was a year-long initiative and has helped save $20M in operational budget.
